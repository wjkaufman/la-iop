Thesis

Shakespeare takes various artistic liberties within King Henry IV Part 1 to present the royalty in a generally positive light and comment on various social and religious events at the time.

King Henry IV is a historical play, so an understanding of the actual history (certainly at the time in which it was written) is crucial to understanding the play as a whole

The History

Richard II (1367-1399)

  • became King of England at age 10
  • John of Gaunt effectively ruled England
  • As Richard matured into power, surrounded himself with unpopular favorites
  • Requests for Parliamentary grants to fight 100 Years' War decreased popularity

Richard II (cont.)

  • Parliament denied grants, created commission to oversee Richard II's activities
  • 1388: Richard II demanded the commission be considered treasonable
  • Lords Appelants outlawed most of Richard's closest friends in Merciless Parliament
  • Richard was subdued until 1398 when he executed several of the Lords Appelant, banished the rest (including Henry of Bolingbroke)

Henry IV (1367-1413)

  • Son of John of Gaunt, earl of Derby (1397-99), duke of Hereford, byname Henry Bolingbroke or Henry of Lancaster
  • Opposed the crown, joined Lords Appelant
  • Richard II seized his inheritence while Henry was banished, then left for campaign in Ireland
  • Henry invaded England, seen as "champion of nobility"
  • Forced Richard II to abdicate, rose to throne in 1399
Try and get through this quickly, because this is information that people already know (to an extent)

Henry V (1386-1422)

  • Son of Henry of Bolingbroke (to be Henry IV), grew up with Richard II
  • became Prince of Wales in 1399 during his father's coronation
  • Showed surprising military skill while a teenager fighting in Battle of Shrewsbury
  • Entered government, often opposed his father and challenged his will
  • While Henry IV's health declined, Prince Henry seized control from Henry IV's favorite Thomas Arundel, Archbishop of Canterbury

Henry V (cont.)

  • Waged an agressive war in France, great military leader
  • Died suddenly of dysentary at the age of 36

Other important history


Sir John Oldcastle (1378-1417)

  • Distinguished soldier, knighted in 1400 by King Henry IV, friends with Prince Henry
  • Member of Lollardy (precursor to Protestant reformation)
  • Served in Parliament, tried to pass anti-clerical reforms as part of Lollard beliefs
  • Archbishop Thomas Arundel saw these reforms, called for Oldcastle to be prosecuted
  • King Henry V tried to protect Oldcastle but eventually allowed Arundel to prosecute, condemn Oldcastle

IV.iii.64-95

Test

“ My father, in kind heart and pity moved,
Swore him assistance, and performed it too.
Now, when the lords and barons of the realm
Perceived Northumberland did lean to him
The more and less came in with cap and knee,
…
In short time after he deposed the King,
Soon after that deprived him of his life,…
To make that worse, suffered his kinsman March —
Who is, if every owner were well placed,
Indeed his King…”
Note how the deposition isn't the issue, only the fact that the Percy family didn't get more power (issues of rightful ruler/king aren't main problem)

Conclusion

Shakespeare used the backdrop of historical events to present his own artistic take on history

  • Presents royalty in positive light
  • Presents Oldcastle negatively to argue against Lollardism or threat to traditional religious/social strucutres
